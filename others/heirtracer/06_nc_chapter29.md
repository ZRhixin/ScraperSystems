# NC Chapter 29 — Intestate Succession Law Reference

**Applies when:** Decedent died domiciled in NC + no valid will + property not distributed by devise.  
**Effective date:** January 1, 1960. Deaths before this date use prior common law → human review.

---

## 5 Required Facts (Must Establish Before Distribution)

1. Date of death
2. Marital status at death (and whether spouse survived 120+ hours)
3. Estate/probate filed? (any open case)
4. Will or intestate? (testacy status)
5. Which family members were alive at time of death?

---

## Distribution Rules

### Step 1: Spouse Share (GS 29-14)

Before distributing to any other heirs, calculate spouse's share:

| Situation | Spouse's Real Property Share |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Spouse + 1 child/descendant | 1/2 undivided interest |
| Spouse + 2+ children/descendants | 1/3 undivided interest |
| Spouse + surviving parent(s), no children | 1/2 undivided interest |
| Spouse only (no children, no parents) | All |

**Dollar floor for personal property (GS 29-14(b)):**
- Deaths before 2012: $30,000 with children / $50,000 with parents
- Deaths 2012+: $60,000 with children / $100,000 with parents
(Spouse gets this floor before children/parents take anything from personal property)

**120-hour survival rule:** Spouse must survive decedent by 120 hours (5 days) to take the intestate share. If both die within 120 hours → treat as spouse predeceased.

### Step 2: Remainder Distribution — Priority Order (GS 29-15)

After spouse's share, remainder (or all if no spouse) passes by this priority:

| Priority | Class | Rule |
|----------|-------|------|
| 1 | Children and their descendants | Split equally among children; deceased child's share goes to their descendants by representation |
| 2 | Parents | Split equally (or all to survivor if one predeceased) |
| 3 | Siblings and their descendants | Same representation rule as class 1 |
| 4 | Grandparents, aunts, uncles | Split: half to paternal branch, half to maternal branch |
| 5 | Collateral kin | Max 5 degrees of kinship. Beyond 5 degrees = escheat to State (GS 29-12) |

**Representation (per stirpes):** When a person in a priority class predeceased the decedent, their share passes to their descendants equally.

## Special Rules

### Half-Blood (GS 29-3)
Half-blood relatives inherit the same as whole-blood relatives. No distinction.

### Adopted Children (GS 29-17)
- Adopted child inherits from adoptive parents (and their kin) as if biological child.
- Adopted child does NOT inherit from biological parents (unless adopted by stepparent who married the natural parent).
- Exception: stepparent adoption — child can still inherit from the natural parent who married the adoptive stepparent.

### Out-of-Wedlock Children (GS 29-19 and GS 49 series)
- Child always inherits from the **mother**.
- Child inherits from the **father** only if paternity is established by one of:
  - Court adjudication (GS 49-1 through 49-9)
  - Paternity action (GS 49-14 through 49-16)
  - Written acknowledgment (GS 52-10b)
  - DNA test with father dead within 1 year
  - Father acknowledged in probated will
- Without paternity establishment: child cannot inherit upward from father's side.

### Slayer Rule (GS 31A)
Person who feloniously kills decedent forfeits all inheritance rights. Treated as if predeceased. Agent should flag for human review.

### Renunciation / Disclaimer (GS 31B)
An heir can disclaim their interest within 9 months of death. Disclaimed share passes as if disclaimant predeceased.

### Advancements (GS 29-24 through 29-28)
Property transferred during lifetime may be treated as advancement against heir's share if written contemporaneous designation exists.

### Simultaneous Death
If two persons die within 120 hours of each other, the one who died first is treated as having predeceased the survivor for inheritance purposes.

### Posthumous Birth (GS 29-9)
Child conceived before death but born within 10 lunar months after → entitled to inherit as if born before death.

---

## Escheat (GS 29-12)

If decedent leaves no heirs within 5 degrees of kinship and no spouse → entire estate escheats to State of NC.

## Dollar Thresholds Summary

| Year of Death | With Children (personal property floor) | With Parents (personal property floor) |
|---------------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Before 2012 | $30,000 | $50,000 |
| 2012 and after | $60,000 | $100,000 |

---

## The Cascade Rule

When an heir has also died:
1. Treat that heir as a new decedent
2. Run the full Chapter 29 analysis again for that person
3. Their share passes to THEIR heirs by the same rules
4. Continue recursively until every branch ends at:
   - A living confirmed person
   - An escheat determination
   - A human review flag
